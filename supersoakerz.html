Super Soaker Gen Z Fan Engagement & Experiential Marketing Strategy

Super Soaker Student-Led Service Marketing Project

Service Execution Proof

The project involved real-time service delivery within a campus parking structure, allowing direct observation of consumer response and peer-driven visibility.

Before Service

After Service

Project Context

Super Soaker was developed as part of an introductory marketing course in which students created and promoted a small service-based business.

The venture focused on washing car windows for fellow students on campus, providing practical experience in localized marketing, service promotion, and consumer engagement within a defined community environment.

Core Insight

College students respond strongly to convenience-based services that reduce small but recurring lifestyle friction, particularly when promoted through peer visibility and campus presence.

Brand Concept

Super Soaker was positioned as a simple, affordable, student-operated service designed to provide quick car window cleaning solutions while building trust through peer-to-peer interaction.

Marketing Objectives

  • Generate awareness of the service among students
  • Communicate convenience and affordability
  • Encourage peer-driven promotion
  • Test localized grassroots marketing tactics

Activation Strategy

Grassroots Campus Promotion
Direct outreach in parking lots and student housing areas to increase visibility.

Flyer-Based Awareness
Simple promotional materials designed for high-traffic campus locations.

Peer Interaction Marketing
Leveraging social proof through visible service execution.

Service Demonstration Effect
Using real-time service delivery as a marketing tool.

Target Persona

Name: Mia, 20
Profile: Full-time college student with busy schedule
Motivation: Convenience and time efficiency
Behavior: Influenced by peer recommendations and visible services
Opportunity: Position service as quick lifestyle solution

Campaign Moodboard

The visual direction reflects a practical, student-run service startup focused on real execution rather than aspirational branding.

Visual themes explored:

  • Campus parking environments
  • Service-based before-and-after transformation cues
  • Simple grassroots promotional aesthetics
  • Peer interaction moments
  • Low-budget startup marketing visuals

This moodboard supports positioning the service as functional, accessible, and community-driven.

Learning Outcomes

  • Applied localized marketing strategy in real-world setting
  • Developed understanding of consumer response to service promotion
  • Gained experience in grassroots marketing execution
  • Observed impact of peer-driven awareness within niche audience

Strategic Takeaway

Small-scale service ventures demonstrate how visibility, convenience messaging, and localized promotion can effectively drive awareness within constrained market environments.
